ABOUT ME

Engineering Precision meets Clinical Empathy

Driven by a passion for Embedded Systems and Biomedical Technology, I specialize in the end-to-end architecture of Critical-Care Medical Devices and Life-Support Systems, ensuring every technology meets the highest standards of regulatory compliance and clinical necessity. Backed by 9+ years of multi-domain experience spanning MedTech, industrial IoT, and embedded security, my core mission is driven by dual leadership: architecting safety-critical embedded systems for critical-care devices at Hild LifeSciences, while simultaneously leading the strategic R&D vision and clinical translation at Inveonix Systems. This comprehensive approach allows me to consistently deliver innovative solutionsfrom ICU ventilators to advanced patient monitoring systems and various medical devices that translate engineering precision into real-world clinical reliability and impact.

My journey in MedTech innovation began with intensive R&D roles at IIT Kanpur, where I spearheaded the development of low-cost, portable, and USB-powered medical devices during the COVID-19 pandemicresulting in the filing and award of 2 Indian Patents for breakthrough medical device designs. This work, conducted in close collaboration with clinicians and regulatory stakeholders, laid the foundation for my commitment to creating technologies that combine engineering precision with clinical empathy and real-world impact.

Indian Patents & Innovations

My professional journey spans diverse domains: I've contributed to industrial IoT solutions at IIT Madras Research Park with Dravak Technologies, developing vibration-based predictive maintenance systems integrated with IBM Cloud; conducted advanced research on Cellular IoT and Embedded Security at ARM Sweden, worked on NB-IoT systems, mbed OS, FreeRTOS, and TrustZone integration for ARM Cortex-M33; and built Embedded Linux Systems at Dynamic Solutions, developing BLE-enabled safety monitoring solutions on BeagleBone Black with Yocto and Android interfaces.
